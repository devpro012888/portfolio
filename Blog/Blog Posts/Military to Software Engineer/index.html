From Military to Software Engineer: My Journey Into Tech

Transition from Military Service to Software Engineer

December 15, 2025

Welcome to The Software Engineer’s Journal, and thank you for reading my very first blog post. My name is Christopher White, and I am currently serving on active duty in the United States Coast Guard. With over 17 years of military service, I’ve built a career rooted in discipline, leadership, and problem-solving—skills that translate naturally into the world of software engineering.

Throughout my Coast Guard career, I’ve worked extensively in administration, pay and personnel, and travel operations. While these roles may not seem technical on the surface, they required attention to detail, process improvement, and critical thinking—all of which align closely with software development.

My passion for technology, however, began long before my military service. Prior to enlisting, I earned an Associate Degree in Computer Information Systems from Atlantic Cape Community College, which laid the foundation for my interest in programming and IT.

While serving in the Coast Guard, I took full advantage of military Tuition Assistance to continue my education:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Information Technology – Purdue Global University
  • Master’s Degree in Information Technology, concentration in Software Development – Southern New Hampshire University

These programs strengthened my understanding of software concepts, systems design, and modern development practices, while reinforcing my goal of transitioning into a full-time software engineering role.

To further sharpen my hands-on skills, I am currently studying through freeCodeCamp.org, where I am working toward a Responsive Web Design Certification. Through this training, I am gaining practical experience building and designing websites using:

  • HTML (HyperText Markup Language)
  • CSS (Cascading Style Sheets)

This learning path is helping me bridge the gap between academic knowledge and real-world software development.

My long-term goal is to secure a position as a software engineer where I can continue learning, growing, and contributing to meaningful projects. While it may not be with a large tech company like Apple, Google, or Microsoft, I am committed to finding an organization that values curiosity, growth, and continuous improvement.

This blog will document my journey from military service to software engineering, including lessons learned, projects I’m working on, and insights for others considering a similar transition. If you’re a fellow service member, veteran, or aspiring developer, I hope my experiences help guide and motivate you.

Thank you for following along on my journey.

Happy coding!
